# squatchy

A Pacific Northwest scene in ASCII art, in the spirit of
[asciiquarium](https://robobunny.com/projects/asciiquarium/).

Two ridgelines of snow-capped mountains, the Sound below them, evergreen
forest, a campsite with a crackling fire — and the wildlife that wanders
through: deer, elk, bears, rabbits, eagles, geese, night owls, leaping
salmon, and orcas that surface to blow like the real thing. If you're
patient, the big guy himself will amble past.

## Run

```sh
./squatchy
```

Needs only Python 3 and a curses-capable terminal (macOS/Linux).
Looks best in a 256-color terminal (falls back to 8 colors gracefully).

## Keys

| key | action |
|-----|--------|
| `q` | quit |
| `p` / space | pause |
| `r` | redraw / regenerate the scene |
| `w` | toggle rain |
| `n` | skip to the next time of day |

## What to watch for

- **Orcas** travel submerged and porpoise: spout first, then the long
  back-and-fin roll, then they slip under. Sometimes one breaches clean
  out of the water.
- **Day fades to night**: the sun and moon arc across the sky, stars
  twinkle, fireflies drift out of the treeline, and the campfire smoke
  keeps rising.
- **Weather rolls in on its own** — it is the PNW. Wind builds, the firs
  sway, rain ramps up, lightning cracks. Mist hangs in the trees after.
- On rare clear nights, look up.

## Easter eggs

Sasquatch sightings are rare and mostly nocturnal legend — check the
treeline, follow the footprints, and mind the eyeshine after dark.
Sometimes he brings family. Impatient cryptozoologists may find certain
keys speed things up.
